Pork Sisig

Sisig is a sizzling Filipino dish made from chopped pork, traditionally using parts like the pig's face and ears, though pork belly is a common and easier substitute.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly or pork mask (maskara)
  • 1 onion, halved (for boiling)
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, smashed (for boiling)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped (for mixing)
  • 2-3 finger chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 3-4 tbsp mayonnaise (optional, for creaminess)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 3-4 calamansi or 1 lemon, juiced
  • 1 tsp chili flakes or chopped bird's eye chili, for heat
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 raw egg (optional, cracked on top before serving)
  • Butter or oil, for searing

Instructions

  1. Boil the pork. In a large pot, combine the pork, onion, tomatoes, and water. Bring to a boil, then skim off the scum that rises to the surface. Lower the heat and simmer for about 45 minutes to an hour, until the pork is tender.
  2. Grill or broil. Grill the boiled pork over charcoal (or broil in the oven) until the skin is crisp and slightly charred, about 10-15 minutes per side. This step adds smokiness and crunch.
  3. Chop finely. Once cooled enough to handle, chop the pork into very small, fine pieces using a knife or cleaver.
  4. Sear the chopped pork. Heat butter or oil in a pan over high heat. Add the chopped pork and sear until slightly crispy on the edges.
  5. Mix in the aromatics. Add the chopped red onion and chilies to the pan, stirring to combine. Season with soy sauce, calamansi or lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  6. Add creaminess (optional). Stir in mayonnaise for a richer, creamier texture, common in many modern sisig versions.
  7. Serve sizzling. Transfer to a sizzling plate if available, crack a raw egg on top, and mix it in tableside so it cooks from the residual heat. Serve immediately with rice and extra calamansi on the side.

Note: For a smokier flavor, some versions also incorporate chopped pork liver or chicharon (pork cracklings) into the mix.
