Chicken Biryani

Chicken biryani is a layered rice dish from South Asia, built from marinated chicken, fragrant basmati rice, and a blend of warm spices.

Ingredients

For the chicken marinade:

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt, to taste
  • Juice of 1 lemon

For the rice:

  • 3 cups basmati rice, soaked for 30 minutes
  • 6 cups water
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • Salt, to taste

For assembly:

  • 3 large onions, thinly sliced and fried until golden (birista)
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh mint
  • 1/4 cup milk, infused with a pinch of saffron
  • 3-4 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit

Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken. Combine the chicken with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, salt, and lemon juice. Mix well and let marinate for at least 1 hour, or overnight in the fridge for best flavor.
  2. Parboil the rice. Bring the water to a boil with the whole spices (cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, bay leaves) and salt. Add the soaked, drained rice and cook until it's about 70% done, still firm at the core. Drain and set aside.
  3. Cook the chicken masala. In a large, heavy-bottomed pot, heat ghee or oil. Add the marinated chicken and cook over medium-high heat until it's about halfway cooked and the masala has thickened, about 10-12 minutes.
  4. Layer the biryani. Spread the chicken masala evenly across the bottom of the pot. Layer the parboiled rice on top. Scatter half the fried onions, cilantro, mint, and slit chilies over the rice, then drizzle with the saffron milk and a little extra ghee.
  5. Seal and steam (dum cooking). Cover the pot tightly with a lid (sealing the edges with dough or foil if needed to trap steam). Cook on very low heat for 20-25 minutes, allowing the rice to finish cooking and absorb the chicken's flavors.
  6. Rest before serving. Let the biryani rest, covered, for 5-10 minutes off the heat. Gently fluff and mix just before serving, garnishing with the remaining fried onions.
  7. Serve. Pair with raita (yogurt sauce), a simple salad, or a boiled egg on the side.

Note: The "dum" technique, sealing the pot to trap steam, is essential for proper biryani; a heavy lid or a layer of foil works if you don't have dough to seal it.
