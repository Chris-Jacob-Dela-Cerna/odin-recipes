Pork Sinigang

Pork sinigang is a Filipino sour soup, typically built around tamarind, with pork belly or ribs simmered with vegetables.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly or pork ribs, cut into chunks
  • 1 packet (40-50g) tamarind soup base (sinigang mix), or fresh tamarind
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2-3 tomatoes, quartered
  • 6-8 cups water
  • 1 radish (labanos), sliced
  • 1 eggplant, sliced
  • A handful of string beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch lengths
  • A bunch of kangkong (water spinach) or spinach
  • 2-3 pieces green finger chilies (siling pansigang)
  • Fish sauce (patis), to taste
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Boil the pork. In a large pot, combine the pork, onion, tomatoes, and water. Bring to a boil, then skim off the scum that rises to the surface. Lower the heat and simmer for about 45 minutes to an hour, until the pork is tender.
  2. Season the broth. Stir in the tamarind soup base (or fresh tamarind pulp), then season with fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste. Let it simmer a few more minutes so the flavors meld.
  3. Add the harder vegetables first. Add the radish and let it cook for about 5 minutes, since it takes longer to soften.
  4. Add the rest of the vegetables. Add the eggplant, string beans, and chilies. Simmer for another 5-7 minutes until the vegetables are just tender but not mushy.
  5. Finish with the greens. Add the kangkong or spinach last and cook for only 1-2 minutes, just until wilted.
  6. Serve. Serve hot with steamed rice, adjusting the sourness or saltiness with extra tamarind mix or fish sauce as needed.

Note: For a deeper sour flavor, some cooks add a splash of fresh tamarind juice or a few slices of unripe tomato or guava at the end.
